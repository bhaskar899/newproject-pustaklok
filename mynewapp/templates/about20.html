The Canterville Ghost

"The Canterville Ghost" by Oscar Wilde is a witty and satirical novella that combines elements of Gothic horror, comedy, and cultural commentary. The story centers around an American family that moves into a haunted British mansion, only to treat the ghost with indifference and humor.

While not a theoretical work, Wilde uses this humorous tale to explore themes such as the clash between American pragmatism and British tradition, redemption, and the humanization of even the supernatural.
