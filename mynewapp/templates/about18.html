Dracula

This is a beautiful hardbound edition of Dracula that comes with gilded edges and elegantly designed endpapers. It is an epistolary novel, written as a collection of realistic but completely fictional diary entries, telegrams, letters, ship’s logs, and newspaper clippings—all of which add a layer of detailed realism to Stoker’s story.

At the time of its publication, Dracula was considered a straightforward horror novel based on imaginary creations of supernatural life. The book is often categorized under vampire literature, horror fiction, gothic novels, and invasion literature.

It explores themes like the role of women in Victorian society, sexual conventions, immigration, colonialism, and post-colonialism. Though Stoker didn’t invent the vampire, he certainly shaped its modern form through this legendary work.
